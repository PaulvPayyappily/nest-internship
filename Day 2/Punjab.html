Welcome to Punjab

Discover the vibrant culture, rich history, and scenic beauty of Punjab.

About Punjab

Punjab, located in the north-western part of India, is known for its rich cultural heritage, vibrant traditions, and agricultural prosperity. The state is often referred to as the "Granary of India" due to its role in the nation's food production. From the sacred Golden Temple in Amritsar to the lush fields of wheat and rice, Punjab is a land of great historical significance, religious diversity, and a bustling, lively spirit.

The Golden Temple

The Golden Temple in Amritsar is one of the most revered religious sites in the world. A symbol of peace, unity, and devotion, it attracts millions of pilgrims every year. The temple's stunning architecture, coupled with the spiritual atmosphere, makes it a must-visit place for anyone traveling to Punjab.

Cultural Heritage

Punjab is known for its lively and energetic culture, reflected in its music, dance, and festivals. Bhangra and Gidda are traditional dance forms that bring people together during celebrations like Lohri, Baisakhi, and Diwali. The cuisine of Punjab is another major highlight, with dishes like butter chicken, sarson da saag, and makki di roti that are loved worldwide.

Agriculture and Landscapes

Punjab is often called the "Granary of India" due to its extensive agricultural output, especially wheat and rice. The fields of Punjab stretch far and wide, offering a serene and scenic landscape. In addition to agriculture, Punjab also has beautiful rural areas, historical sites, and vibrant cities that showcase its rich diversity.
